Protecting mobile devices from cyber threats

Types of Cyber Threats

  • Malware (Malicious Software): Phishing is when scammers send fake messages to trick you into giving personal info, clicking harmful links, or downloading viruses.
  • Phishing & Social Engineeringn: Mobile malware is harmful software that targets phones through bad apps, sites, or emails. It can steal data and take control of your device.
  • Ransomware : Ransomware is a type of malware that locks your files or device and demands money to unlock them.
  • Data Breaches : When sensitive information is accessed or leaked without permission.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks : When a hacker secretly intercepts and alters communication between two parties.

    How to Protect Our Mobile Devices from Cyber Threats

    Use Strong Passwords or Biometrics Use complex PINs, passwords, or fingerprint/face recognition.
    Keep Software Updated Always install the latest system and app updates for security patches.
    Install Apps Only from Trusted Sources Download apps from official stores like Google Play or Apple App Store.
    Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Add an extra layer of security to your accounts.
    Use Antivirus or Security Apps These can help detect and block threats.
